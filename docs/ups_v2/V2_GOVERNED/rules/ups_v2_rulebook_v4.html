1. About This Constitution

UPS Salary Cap Dynasty is a 12-franchise, full-contract dynasty league that has operated continuously since 2010. This constitution is the single authoritative reference for how every franchise is managed — from roster construction and contract obligations to transaction windows and league governance.

Purpose and authority

This document governs all rules not administered automatically by the MFL platform. Where platform settings and this constitution conflict, the Commissioner will adjudicate. The Commissioner enforces this constitution in good faith and in the best interest of competitive fairness. Where the constitution is silent on a matter, the Commissioner may rule directly or put the question to a league vote.

Rule changes. Minor rule changes require a unanimous Competition Committee vote, or a 51% league vote if the CC is not unanimous. In-season changes require a 90% threshold. Changes take effect at the start of the season in which they are adopted — not mid-season — unless the vote itself specifies otherwise.

What this constitution covers

Thirteen sections govern the complete lifecycle of franchise ownership — from how rosters are built and how contracts are priced, to how transactions are processed, how trades work, and how the league is run from February through the end of the season each December. Every contract rule includes a worked dollar example so owners can verify their own math.

2. League Overview & Format

UPS Salary Cap Dynasty is a 12-franchise, full-IDP, salary cap dynasty league hosted on MyFantasyLeague (League ID 74598). The league has operated continuously since 2010 and runs under a hard salary cap, a structured contract system, and a live application that tracks all contracts, transactions, and deadlines.

League at a glance

Franchises
12 franchises organized into 4 divisions of 3. Each franchise is owned and managed by a single owner.
Platform
MyFantasyLeague (MFL), League ID 74598. MFL handles lineup locks, scoring, waiver processing, and auction mechanics. This constitution governs everything not administered by the platform.
Salary cap
$300,000.49 hard cap, enforced in-season. No cap limit during the off-season. A cap floor of $260,000 must be reached by auction completion or the Contract Deadline Date, whichever comes first.
Annual dues
$200 per franchise. First installment ($100) is due before the Free Agent Auction. Second installment ($100) is due by the Thanksgiving trade deadline. Payments are made via Venmo to @Keith-Creelman. Late payments incur a $3,000 cap penalty for each week overdue.

Division realignment

Divisions are realigned every three years. The realignment cycle follows the years 2026, 2029, 2032, and so on. The formula used to determine which franchises land in which division is currently under review for the 2026 realignment — the updated methodology will be published before realignment takes effect.

Historical realignment results and the full methodology history will be documented in a separate appendix.

Commissioner and governance

Commissioner
Keith Creelman. The Commissioner manages the day-to-day operation of the league, enforces this constitution, resolves disputes, and handles administrative tasks that do not require a vote. The Commissioner may act unilaterally on administrative and logistical matters; established rules require a league vote to change.
Competition Committee
A three-person body — Keith Creelman, Ryan Bousquet, and Eric Mannila — that evaluates and proposes rule changes each off-season. The Committee reviews proposals annually in April, and changes adopted at that meeting take effect for the upcoming season only. In-season rule changes require a higher threshold. A formal voting process for proposals that go beyond the Committee will be established and documented here when finalized.
Formal governance process — pending documentation AMB-001

The specific voting thresholds, proposal submission process, and owner participation requirements are being formalized. Until published, the Commissioner will handle all rule proposals through the Competition Committee and league vote on a case-by-case basis.

Departing owners and dispersal

When an owner departs or a franchise becomes available, a Dispersal Draft governs the transition. The departing franchise's assets are pooled and made available via a snake draft conducted live on Discord. The Commissioner sets the draft order and facilitates the process. Incoming owners may contribute assets as negotiated.

Owners who fail to actively manage their roster, submit lineups, or meet financial obligations may face commissioner action up to and including franchise reassignment. See Section 12 for the full penalty schedule.

3. Season Calendar & Key Deadlines

After the fantasy playoffs close in early January, the league enters a brief off-season through February. The operational year restarts March 1 — contracts roll forward, the tag and extension windows open, and the off-season transaction calendar gets underway. Most deadlines are anchored to NFL events rather than fixed calendar dates, so they shift slightly each year while remaining predictable.

Source of record. Dates marked widget override come directly from the live league countdown widget. Dates marked schedule-derived are calculated from the 2026 NFL schedule file. Where neither source has a confirmed value, the entry is flagged for Commissioner confirmation rather than estimated.

Off-season — New league year through Rookie Draft weekend

New league year opens · ~March 1
All contracts advance one year. Rookie contracts entering their final year become expiring. Dead cap carries into the new year. The league application refreshes all contract statuses. The Commissioner may extend the processing window to March 15 if system updates require it.
Tag window closes · Memorial Day minus 4 days · 2026: May 21, 12:00 PM ET
Each owner may tag up to one offensive player and one defensive/special teams player from their roster. Only expired non-rookie contracts are tag-eligible. Tagged players are retained and their salary is calculated based on where they ranked among their position group during the prior season (Weeks 1–17 UPS total points). The tag window closes 4 days before Memorial Day Sunday. See Section 10 for full tag rules and salary tier math.
Rookie extension deadline · Same as tag deadline · 2026: May 21, 12:00 PM ET
This is the final date for an owner to extend a player whose rookie contract is expiring. After this deadline, unextended expiring rookies are placed into the Expired Rookie Auction. See Section 5 for extension rules.
Expired Rookie Auction · Tied to Rookie Draft weekend · 2026: ⚑ date TBC
Expired rookie contracts that were not extended are placed into the Expired Rookie Auction. These players receive a regular auction-style contract, comparable to a Free Agent Auction player. They cannot be cut prior to the Contract Deadline Date. The auction is conducted near Rookie Draft weekend; the 2026 Expired Rookie Auction start date is not yet confirmed in the widget — flag for Commissioner confirmation.
Rookie Draft · Sunday of Memorial Day weekend · 2026: May 24, 6:30 PM ET
The annual rookie draft is held live on Discord, always on the Sunday of Memorial Day weekend. All owners must be present in person, reachable by cell phone, or connected through the Discord livestream. Rounds 1–5 order is based on prior season playoff results. Round 6 is randomized. See Section 6 for full draft rules.

Auction & pre-season — Cut-down through Week 1

Roster cut-down day · 2026: July 29, 12:00 PM ET
Owners must reduce their roster to within legal limits (active + taxi + IR) before the Free Agent Auction opens. Players released during cut-down become available at the auction. First dues installment ($100) must be paid before an owner may participate in the auction.
Free Agent Auction starts · Last weekend of July (may slide to early August) · 2026: July 31, 12:00 PM ET
All unsigned players not on a roster are available in the annual live auction. The auction starts on the last weekend of July and typically runs 10–12 active days. It may slide into early August if significant owner availability conflicts arise. Recent history: 2023 ran July 29 – Aug 8 (11 days); 2024 ran July 27 – Aug 7 (12 days); 2025 ran Aug 3 – Aug 13 (11 days). See Section 7 for full auction rules.
Contract Deadline Date · Last Sunday before NFL Week 1 · 2026: September 6
By this date, all of the following must be filed and processed:
  • Auction contracts longer than 1 year
  • Contract restructures
  • Pre-season waiver players receiving multi-year contracts
  • Extensions for players already under contract who are entering their final year
Any contract not on file by this deadline reverts to a 1-year deal at the bid or pick-up price. The cap floor ($260,000) must also be reached by this date. See Section 5 for contract rules.
NFL Week 1 · 2026: September 10
The regular season begins. Lineups lock at individual NFL game kickoff times. In-season transaction rules take effect for all roster moves made from this point forward.
Pre-season MYM deadline · Before Week 3 opens · 2026: September 24 (widget override; Week 3 Thursday kickoff is 8:15 PM ET same day)
Owners have until this date to file a Mid-Year Multi (MYM) contract on any player acquired during the pre-season window (before Week 1). The deadline falls on the same calendar day as the Week 3 Thursday night opener — file before kickoff. After this deadline, pre-season acquisitions that were not contracted are locked at a 1-year deal. See Section 8 for MYM rules.
In-season extension window closes · Before Week 5 opens · 2026: October 7 (widget override; Week 4 ends ~Oct 4–5, Week 5 opens Oct 8)
There are two groups of players eligible for extensions after Week 1:
  • Players already under contract before the auction who are entering the final year of that contract — owners have until October 7 to extend.
  • Players acquired at auction or on pre-season waivers who were not already under a prior contract — owners also have until October 7 to file a multi-year deal, which is separate from the pre-season MYM window.
After October 7, no extensions may be processed until the following off-season.

In-season — Weeks 1 through season complete

Second dues installment · Due by the trade deadline
The second $100 installment is due no later than the Thanksgiving trade deadline. Owners more than one week late incur a $3,000 cap penalty for each week overdue.
In-season MYM deadline · 2 weeks after acquisition
A player added during the season — via waivers, free agency, or trade — must receive a Mid-Year Multi (MYM) contract within 2 weeks of landing on the roster, or they revert to a 1-year deal at the pick-up price. The 2-week clock starts from the date the player was added. See Section 8 for full MYM rules.
Trade deadline · Kickoff of the first NFL Thanksgiving game · 2026: November 26
No trades — players, picks, or cap adjustments — may be processed after the kickoff of the first NFL Thanksgiving Day game. The window closes at the exact moment of kickoff. Trades pending acceptance at kickoff are voided. The 2026 exact kickoff time should be confirmed against the NFL schedule once released; the date of November 26 is confirmed from the league database. See Section 10 for full trade rules.
Regular season ends · NFL Week 14
The final week of regular season play. Playoff seedings are determined by record, then all-play record as a tiebreaker. Division winners earn automatic playoff berths.
Playoffs · NFL Weeks 15–17
The UPS postseason runs across NFL Weeks 15–17. Six teams qualify: the 4 division winners plus the next 2 best records. The bracket plays out over three weeks, with Week 17 serving as the final week of the tournament. See Section 11 for full playoff format and seeding rules.
UPS Bowl & HawkTuah Bowl · NFL Week 17 (final week of playoffs)
Week 17 is the final week of the UPS postseason. The UPS Bowl is the league championship game between the two remaining playoff teams. The HawkTuah Bowl is the consolation game between the two teams eliminated in the Week 16 semifinals. Both games are scored on the standard MFL platform within the same Week 17 window.
Season complete · Early January
Week 17 spans December 31, 2026 through January 4, 2027. After all Week 17 scoring is finalized, the Commissioner distributes payouts and publishes final standings. January and February are the off-season. The new league year begins March 1.
