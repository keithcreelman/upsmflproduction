Win-Impact Score — How We Grade Every Weekly Performance

Every player-week is scored relative to what a typical starter at that position produced that season. A week is Elite if it was clearly a league-winning performance, Plus if it beat the median starter, Neutral if it was about league-average, and Dud if it actively hurt your week.

Technically: z = (points − p50_starter) / (p80 − p50), using only weeks where the player was actually rostered-starter somewhere in MFL. Buckets:

Week Tierz-scoreWhat it means
Elite≥ 1.0Top-20% starter week — single-handedly swings your matchup
Plus0.25 – 1.0Above the median starter — solid contribution
Neutral−0.5 – 0.25Around median — neither helped nor hurt
Dud< −0.5Cost you the matchup — worse than 80% of starters

Where it's used: Per-week tier badges in the player profile game log. Aggregated as Elite% / Plus% / E+P% / Dud% in Career Summary.

Rookie Tier — NET Score (E+P minus ½×Dud)

Each rookie's 3-year games-weighted NET score drives their tier label:

NET = (3yr E+P rate) − 0.5 × (3yr Dud rate)

TierNET scoreWhat it means
Smash≥ +30Roster cornerstone — thrilled to have drafted

Translation: Hitting E+P weeks (elite + plus performances) and avoiding dud weeks explain most of the variance in AP%. Offense does more of the heavy lifting than defense (~74% vs 26%), but elite defense still unlocks a real ~10-point jump in AP above a quality threshold. Draft Rating — How We Score Owners on Draft Hits

Smashing on 1.01 is easy. Smashing on 5.11 is almost impossible. Draft Rating corrects for that by measuring each pick's NET score relative to what a median pick at that slot historically produces:

Draft Rating (per pick) = actual NET − slot-expected NET

Then averaged across an owner's picks (positive = consistently outperforms their slots, negative = underperforms). Late-round smashes get outsized credit automatically because the expected NET for late slots hovers near zero.

Where it's used: team card row on Team Tendencies ("Draft Rating"). Color-coded: green = +5 or better, red = −5 or worse.
