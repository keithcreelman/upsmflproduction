Off-Season Extensions
Begins February 1st until September 1st.
1 Year Contract Extension
Extend the contract 1 year in addition to the upcoming season.  Contract year extends to 2 (two).
Must give the player a raise of a minimum based on chart below for each position or 20% of the player’s AVERAGE salary from current contract (whichever is greater)
AVERAGE = Total Salary/Length of Original Contract
Offensive Players (QB, RB, WR & TE) $10,000
Defensive Players (DL, LB, DB) $3,000
Special Teams (K, PN) $2,000
The player’s raise is effective immediately and applies to both of the remaining years.
2 Year Contract Extension
Extend the contract 2 years more years in addition to the upcoming season.  Contract year extends to 3 (three).
Must give the player a raise of a minimum based on chart below for each position or 40% of the player’s AVERAGE salary from current contract (whichever is greater)
AVERAGE = Total Salary/Length of Original Contract
Offensive Players (QB, RB, WR & TE) $20,000
Defensive Players (DL, LB, DB) $6,000
Special Teams (K, PN) $5,000
The player’s raise is effective immediately and applies to all of the remaining years.

Franchise and Transitional Tags
Franchise and Transition players are named on June 1st.  At this time, the team owner must tag any potential free agents on their team, players with (0) zero remaining contract years, with a qualifying offer. All tags and bids must be posted to the league’s message board to be considered official.
Any potential free agents not tagged on or before June 1st become unrestricted free agents immediately and are eligible for the free agent auction.
Open bidding on tagged players begins on June 2nd and ends on June 15th via league message board.
Franchise Tag Specifics
Only 1 Player per team may be tagged with “Franchise Tag”
Minimum salary offer must be equal more or equal to the top 5 average of the position and must exceed player’s current contract.
During open bidding, a team may outbid the initial qualifying offer for the rights to this player.
If at the end of the franchise period, the qualifying offer IS NOT outbid, the original owner has 2 options.
They may retain that player’s rights for 1 year at the Qualifying offer price.  Player will become a free-agent the following season.
They may cut the player prior to the Free-Agent Auction Draft with no Cap Penalty.
If at the end of the franchise period, the qualifying offer IS outbid, the original owner has 2 options:
Match the highest offer made by the end of the day on June 17th.  This will allow owner to retain player for 1 year at the matched Salary
Allow the player to “walk” and receive compensation from the team that had the highest bid.
Compensation for a Franchised Tagged Player is Two (2) first round draft picks.
In most cases this will result in the current season as well as the following season’s pick to be relinquished to the owner who lost the player.
In the event the owner who won the bid on the Franchise Player has two (2) first round picks in the upcoming draft, the team that let the player “walk” can decide what picks they want to receive if the player that they let “walk” ended the prior season in the Top 5 in Total Points for their respective position.
In other words, Team A lets a player “walk” who was the 5th highest rated player (or better) in his position the previous season, to Team B who has the 1st pick overall as well as Pick 10.  In this scenario, Team A can choose both picks from this upcoming season or can choose 1 pick in current season and 1 pick the following season.
If Team A lets a player “walk” who was ranked 6th or lower, Team B decides what picks he will relinquish.
Unless a team has the required compensation necessary to compensate an owner, they will be prohibited from placing any such bid on a “Franchise Tagged Player”
If player is allowed to walk, the team that has won the “Franchise Tagged” player has the right to offer said a player a multiple year contract prior to Free-Agent Auction Draft.
Transition Tag Specifics
Only 1 Player per team may be tagged with “Transition Tag”
Minimum salary offer must be equal more or equal to the top 5 average of the position and must exceed player’s current contract.
During open bidding, a team may over bid the initial qualifying offer for the rights to this player.
If at the end of the franchise period, the qualifying offer is not outbid, the original owner has 2 options.
They may retain that player’s rights for 1 year at the Qualifying offer price.  Player will become a free-agent the following season.
They may cut the player prior to the Free-Agent Auction Draft with no Cap Penalty.
If at the end of the franchise period, the qualifying offer is outbid, the original owner has 2 options:
Match the highest offer made by the end of the day on June 17th.  This will allow owner to retain player for 1 year at the matched Salary
Allow the player to “walk” and receive compensation from the team that had the highest bid.
Compensation for a Transition Tagged Player is a 2nd and a 3rd round pick (Must be from current season cannot be deferred)
In the event the owner who won the bid on the Transition Player has two (2) 2nd round picks or two (2) 3rd round picks in the upcoming draft, the team that let the player “walk” can decide what picks they want to receive if the player that they let “walk” ended the prior season in the Top 10 in Total Points for their respective position.
In other words, Team A lets a player “walk” who was the 10th highest rated player (or better) in his position the previous season, to Team B who has the 1st pick in the 2nd as well as the 3rd pick in the 2nd.  In this scenario, Team A can choose the earlier pick.
If Team A lets a player “walk” who was ranked 11th or lower, Team B decides what picks he will relinquish.
If player is allowed to walk, the team that has won the “Transition Tagged” player has the right to offer said a player a multiple year contract prior to Free-Agent Auction Draft.
Unless a team has the required compensation necessary to compensate an owner, they will be prohibited from placing any such bid on a “Franchise Tagged Player”
Franchise and transition players may not be traded during this franchise and transition tag bidding period.
Only teams with the required draft choice compensation are eligible for bidding on franchise and transition players.
Front and Back Loaded Contracts
Maximum allowed on a roster at any point is 5
Can be applied to Contract Extensions and to Free Agent Auction Draft Contracts
Does not apply to Rookie Contracts
Front Loaded Structure:
2 Yr Contract (65% Year 1, 35% Year 2)
Example:  Win Arian Foster in Free Agent Auction Draft for $65K (Roughly Highest Salary in 2011)
Total Contract = $65K * 2 = $130K total
Year 1 85K ($130K * 65%) =$84.5K Rounded up to $85K
Year 2 45K (130K* 35%) = $45.5K Take Total Salary $130 less amount Paid ($85K) = $45K (Not rounded up)
3 Yr Contract (50% Year 1, 35% Year 2 & 15% Year 3)
Example:  Win Arian Foster in Free Agent Auction Draft for $65K (Roughly Highest Salary in 2011)
Total Contract = $65K * 3 = $195K total
Year 1 98K ($195K * 50%) =$97.5K Rounded up to $98K
Year 2 68K (195K* 35%) = $68.25K rounded down to $68K
Year 3 $29K (195K *15%) = 29.25K Total Salary $195K-Total Paid $166K = $29K
Back Loaded Structure:
2 Yr Contract (35% Year 1, 65% Year 2)
Example:  Win Arian Foster in Free Agent Auction Draft for $65K (Roughly Highest Salary in 2011)
Total Contract = $65K * 2 = $130K total
Year 1 46K ($130K * 35%) =$45.5K Rounded up to $46K
Year 2 84K (130K* 65%) = $84.5K Take Total Salary $130 less amount Paid ($46K) = $84K (Not rounded up)
3 Yr Contract (15% Year 1, 35% Year 2 & 50% Year 3)
Example:  Win Arian Foster in Free Agent Auction Draft for $65K (Roughly Highest Salary in 2011)
Total Contract = $65K * 3 = $195K total
Year 1 29K ($195K * 15%) =$29.25 Rounded down to $29K
Year 2 68K (195K* 35%) = $68.25K rounded down to $68K
Year 3 $98K (195K *50%) = $97.5K Total Salary $195K-Total Paid $97K = $98K
Transactions
Cutting Players
Any player cut whose TOTAL SALARY is GREATER than $4000 will be subject to a 20% cap penalty based on TOTAL SALARY REMAINING.
Total Salary = Salary due to player.   For a normal contract this is simply Contract Years remaining on Contract * Salary per year.
For Special Front or Back Loaded Contracts this is the total Salary still owed to player.
Penalties accumulate over the course of the season and off-season up until the Free-Agent Auction Draft
The Cap Penalty will be applied at the start of the off-season.
February 1st
The Cap Penalty will be rounded either up or down to the nearest whole thousand prior to Free- Agent Auction Draft.
Any player cut after the start of the Free Agent Auction Draft who is subject to a cap penalty will be applied to the following season’s Cap.
Example of typical Cap Penalty:
Tom Brady signs a 2 year $50K contract and is cut during season 1.  He will be subject to a 20% hit on 2 years @ 50K or $20K hit.
If Tom is cut after February 1st, his contract will have rolled forward and now is only owed 1 year @ $50K or a $10K hit
Any player cut during the off-season with remaining years left on contract is NOT ELIGIBLE to be bid on by the owner who cut said player during the Free-Agent Auction Draft.
This is to enforce the importance of making sound decisions with the players that you sign.
Special CIRCUMSTANCE (Hold-Outs)
If a player on a roster is holding out as of September 1st, that player will receive a 25% Cap Relief.
Once player returns from hold-out relief is removed.
If player is still holding out as of Week 8, the owner have 2 options
If owner wants contract to remain as is and roll-forward at end of year.
CAP Relief remains at 25% until player returns to NFL
Player takes up a roster spot
Contract rolls 1 year forward in February
If owner would like players’ contract to freeze and not count as a year.
Player gets FULL CAP Relief
A Roster Spot is Cleared up.
Player gets dropped to “Limbo” where he will be locked for the remainder of year and CC will place back on roster after Contracts Roll Forward
Player is NOT ELIGIBLE to be used the remainder of this season.
Contract years do not change the following season.
Suspensions, Retires, Jail Birds
Any player who gets suspended for an entire/remainder of season, retires, or goes to jail while locked up under contract may be cut with no cap penalty.
Owner relinquishes rights to this player.
Owner may retain these players if they like with a 50% Cap Relief.
Player still takes up a roster spot
Cap Relief goes away once player returns to NFL.
Contract rolls forward if incident occurred in-season.
Contract does not roll-forward if incident occurred in off-season.
This only applies if the player was under contract prior to one of these special circumstances.  If A.Foster goes to Jail for a year, and is a free-agent, you will not receive the relief noted in this section once you sign him to a deal.
