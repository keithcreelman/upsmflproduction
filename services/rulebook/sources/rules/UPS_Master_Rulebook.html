1. League Overview

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

1.1 Overview

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

The UPS Salary Cap Dynasty League is designed to offer a dynamic and competitive fantasy football experience, combining the strategic depth of salary cap management with the long-term planning required in a dynasty format. This league fosters a community of dedicated owners who are committed to fair play, strategic thinking, and a relentless pursuit of championship glory.

1.2 League Structure

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Teams and Divisions:

The league consists of 12 teams, divided into 4 divisions.

Divisions are realigned every 3 years based on each team's historical all-play record.

Division Assignments:

Division 1: 1/8/9 All-Play

Division 2: 2/7/10 All-Play

Division 3: 3/6/11 All-Play

Division 4: 4/5/12 All-Play

New owners entering the league during a realignment year are assigned an All-Play record of .500 and then slotted accordingly. If multiple new owners enter the league simultaneously, a random generator will be used to determine the tiebreaker for All-Play assignment.

Owners taking over a roster mid-season or after the Free Agent Auction has begun will not have that season's record count toward All-Play.

Future Realignment Years: 2026, 2029, 2032, 2035, etc.

Playoff Format:

The league features two sets of playoffs: the UPS Championship and the Hawktuah Bowl (Toilet Bowl).

Playoff seeding is determined by regular-season performance, with division winners guaranteed a top seed.

Scoring System:

The league utilizes a custom scoring system tailored to reward strategic depth and player performance. Details of the scoring system are outlined in [Insert Section Number].

1.3 Expectation of owners

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Owner Expectations:

All league members are expected to be active participants throughout the season and the off-season. This includes:

Regular communication via the Discord App.

Timely responses to trade offers.

Participation in league events, including the Rookie Draft and Free Agent Auction.

Maintaining awareness of league rules and important league matters.

Active Participation:

Actions that attempt to circumvent the competitive balance of the league, such as collusion or other maneuvers, will be grounds for league dismissal.

1.4 League Membership

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Admission Process:

New members must complete an admission process, including a baseline quiz to ensure a solid understanding of the league's unique structure and value system. This league differs significantly from standard redraft and dynasty formats, requiring time and effort to grasp fully.

The commissioner will subjectively review the results and present them to the league for approval.

Probation Period:

New members are on probation for their first two full seasons.

The league will assess the new member's progress in understanding the rules and value. At the end of the probation period, the league will vote on granting full membership.

Early Termination:

During probation, the league can vote to remove a new member if they fail to demonstrate sufficient growth in understanding or if their participation jeopardizes league balance.

Repeated penalties can also result in early termination of membership.

Dispersal Draft for New Owners:

When a new owner joins the league, existing teams may choose to throw all of their assets (players and future eligible draft picks, excluding 6th round picks) into a player pool to be combined with the new owner's assets.

A random generator will determine the snake draft order, and a draft will take place within Discord for all assets in the pool.

Once a team commits to the dispersal draft, they cannot withdraw.

It is crucial that new owners fully understand the league rules before committing, as they will immediately compete with other owners.

The commissioner always acts in the best interest of the league, ensuring fair play and maintaining competitive balance.

3.5 Super Flex Rules

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Maximum Number of Starting QBs:

Beginning with the contract deadline date, all teams are only allowed to carry three (3) starting QBs on their roster at any time.

This limit includes any QB on the active roster, as well as those promoted from the taxi squad who become starters.

Exceptions:

If a QB on the taxi squad becomes a starter after Week 1, they do not count against the 3 QB maximum.

If a QB is promoted from the taxi squad during the season and becomes a starter, they will count against the 3 QB maximum, regardless of their Week 1 status.

Monitoring Compliance:

The league will monitor compliance using depth charts from sources like FantasyPros Depth Charts.

If a team has more than 3 starting QBs on their roster, they must get in compliance by the kickoff of the following Thursday's game. Non-compliance will result in the commissioner dropping the player that created the 4th QB on the roster.

5. Free Agent Auction

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

5.1 Auction Overview

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Auction Timing:

The Free Agent Auction typically begins on the last weekend of July and lasts approximately one week.

This is a mandatory league event, and compliance with auction rules is strictly monitored.

Roster and Cap Requirements:

Maximum Roster Size During Auction: 35 players.

Minimum Roster Size by Completion of Auction: 27 players.

Valid Lineup Requirement: You must be able to submit a valid starting lineup by the completion of the auction.

Spending Flexibility:

The auction system provides a dashboard indicating the maximum amount you can spend to fill your roster.

For example, the system might show "Max you can spend to fill your roster is $20K," assuming you are filling all 35 roster spots. However, if you only need the minimum of 27 players, you may have additional spending flexibility, potentially increasing this amount (e.g., to $28K).

It is the owner's responsibility to avoid overbidding during the auction. The system prevents you from spending more than $300K, but it will not account for the minimum roster requirement.

Bidding Process:

The auction follows an "Ebay Proxy Style," where each bid needs to remain the highest for 36 hours to win the player.

Owners are expected to nominate players within a 24-hour period. Missing a nomination incurs escalating penalties, starting at $3K and increasing with each offense.

5.2 Auction Roster Lock Date

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

3 Days Prior to Free Agent Auction Start:

This is the deadline for cutting players before the auction begins. After this date, rosters are locked until the auction is complete.

5.3 Auction Floor

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Salary Cap Floor:

The salary cap floor is set at $260K, which must be met by the completion of the auction or by the Contract Deadline Date.

Maintaining the Cap Floor:

Once the floor is reached, if a trade or IR designation occurs that results in a lower salary, the team is still considered compliant.

Owners if eligible may restructure a contract and front-load it to meet this minimum.

Objective:

The goal is to ensure owners are engaged during the auction and not simply "sleepwalking" through it. Providing the option to front-load a contract offers an alternative to forcing teams to bid on players they don't want, while still requiring active roster management. Failing to adhere to this rule indicates a general failure by the owner to put their team in a position to succeed.

Refer to the Penalties section of the rulebook for non-compliance issues.

7.1 Overview

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Waiver System:

The waiver system allows teams to acquire players who are not currently on any roster. There are two primary types of waivers used in the league: Blind Bid Waivers and First-Come, First-Serve (FCFS) Free Agency.

7.2 Blind Bid Waivers

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Waiver Runs:

Blind Bid Waivers run on Thursday, Friday, Saturday, and Sunday at 9 AM during the NFL season.

Players are locked after each run until the next waiver period begins.

Submitting Bids:

Teams submit conditional blind bids where they can prioritize multiple waiver claims. The highest bid for each player wins the waiver claim.

The blind bid winning amount becomes the player's salary for the current season.

Tiebreakers:

If two teams submit identical bids for the same player, the tiebreaker process is as follows:

All Play Record

Overall Record

Total Points

Head-to-Head Record

During the preseason and up until the completion of Week 1, a custom order based on the prior season's final draft slot finish is used for tiebreakers.

Bid Processing:

The system processes bids top to bottom within each group and accepts the first valid bid, ignoring the rest in that group.

The groups are processed without priority over one another, ensuring all bids are evaluated equally.

7.3 First-Come, First-Serve (FCFS) Free Agency

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

FCFS Availability:

After the Sunday morning waiver run, free agency opens on a first-come, first-serve basis.

Players can be picked up freely until the kickoff of their respective games.

Player Salary:

Any player acquired via FCFS will have a $1K salary amount for the current season.

The default contract length for FCFS acquisitions is 1 year.

Purpose:

FCFS ensures that teams can always field a starting lineup, even if they missed a player during the waiver runs.

7.4 Waiver Compliance and Responsibilities

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Roster Compliance:

Owners are responsible for ensuring their roster complies with league rules after making waiver acquisitions.

Teams must have enough roster space and cap room to accommodate players acquired through waivers.

Contract Terms:

The default number of years for a waiver and FCFS acquisition is 1 year unless otherwise stated.

Trading is allowed during the entire offseason and up until the NFL's Thanksgiving week, which marks the league's trade deadline.

After the trade deadline, no trades are permitted until the start of the offseason.

8.2 Eligibility

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Eligible Players:

Any player with 1 or more years remaining on their contract is eligible to be traded.

Expired rookies can be traded up until the extension deadline. Other expired contracts are not eligible to be traded.

Draft Picks:

All draft picks for the current draft year, as well as picks for 1 year into the future, are available for trade.

Exception: Round 6 picks are not eligible for trade.

Salary:

Salary can also be included in trades. Please refer to the Cap Adjustments section for detailed rules on trading salary [link to Cap Adjustments section].

8.3 Additional Trade Rules

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Compliance:

All trades require both teams to immediately comply with roster standards. If a trade results in a team having fewer than the minimum required players for a valid starting lineup or total roster, the team must take immediate action to get back into compliance.

If a team is out of compliance, the system will prevent them from submitting a valid lineup, which carries its own penalty.

If a trade results in any contracts being out of compliance (such as exceeding contract limits or requiring adjustments), those changes must be made within 24 hours. Failure to correct contract discrepancies within this timeframe could result in penalties.

Trade Assets Requirement:

Teams are required to include at least some asset (not salary) in every trade. This rule was implemented to avoid asset-less trades, which caused dissatisfaction among league members.

This rule ensures that even during the auction, trades include tangible assets, preventing awkward transactions where players are simply dumped due to roster or budget constraints.

Trade Review Process:

All trades are processed immediately to ensure timely processing.

All trades are subject to review to ensure they fall within guidelines and may be subject to reversal.

Extensions and Salary Compliance:

If a player involved in a trade is eligible for an extension, the team trading the player away can agree to apply the extension as part of the trade. This must be stated in the trade comments, or proof of discussion must be provided before the trade is approved.

All teams involved in the trade must ensure salary compliance according to the league's salary cap rules.

9.1 Glossary of Contract Terms

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Contract Length:

The length of a contract in years from the time the contract was submitted.

Salary:

The current year's salary for a player.

Total Contract Value (TCV):

The total value of a contract, calculated by multiplying the contract length by the Average Annual Value (AAV).

Average Annual Value (AAV):

The average salary per year if the total contract value is evenly distributed across the contract length. This is used as the baseline for all contract extensions.

Years Remaining:

The number of seasons remaining for a player under contract.

9.2 Contract Types

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Contract Types:

Veteran Contracts:

Default contract type for players acquired in the Expired Rookie Auction, Free Agent Auction, or Waivers before the contract deadline date.

Contracts are evenly distributed across all contract years.

Default Contract Length is 1

Waiver Wire (WW) Contracts:

Default contract type for players picked up off waivers after the contract deadline date.

Contract length is always 1 year.

Rookie Contracts:

Applies to NFL rookies acquired via the Rookie Draft, Free Agent Auction, or waivers.

Waiver wire pickups will take on rookie status at the completion of the season if still on the roster or at the time of any in-season contract submission.

Contract Length is always set at 3 for players selected in the Rookie Draft otherwise the default is .

Front-Loaded Contracts:

Contracts where the first year's salary is more than the AAV (Average Annual Value).

Can only be applied to players acquired in the Free Agent Auction, Expired Rookie Auction, or submitted as part of an offseason contract restructure.

The contract split must equal the TCV at the completion of the split.

Example: A 3-year contract with a TCV of $90K could be split as $40K in Year 1, $30K in Year 2, and $20K in Year 3.

Back-Loaded Contracts:

Contracts where the first year's salary is less than the AAV.

Can only be given to players acquired in the Free Agent Auction or Expired Rookie Auction as part of a multi-year contract, or as part of an offseason contract restructure.

A minimum of 20% of the TCV must be allocated to Year 1.

Example: A 3-year contract with a TCV of $90K must have at least $18K allocated to Year 1 (20% of $90K). The remaining amount can be split across the remaining years.

9.3 Contract Minimums and Maximums

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Maximum Salary:

$300,000 (No maximum in the off-season up until Roster Cut Date).

Maximum 3-Year Contracts:

6 contracts (Excludes 3-Year Rookie Contracts).

Maximum Loaded Contracts:

5 contracts (includes Front or Back Loaded contracts).

9.4 Contract Transactions

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Auction Contracts:

Any player acquired in the Free Agent Auction, Expired Rookie Auction, or via waivers before the Contract Deadline Date is eligible for a Multi-Year Auction Contract.

Contract Options:

The contract may be given as a 2-year or 3-year deal.

It can be structured as a Veteran Contract with even distribution or as a Loaded Contract.

Default Contract:

If no Multi-Year Auction Contract is submitted, the default contract length is 1 year.

Contract Type Default: Veteran or Rookie (if the player is given a MYM in their rookie year).

Mid-Year Multi (MYM):

Converts 1-Year Contracts into a Multi-Year Contract at the same salary.

Teams are allowed a maximum of 3 MYMs per season.

If the contract is a WW contract, it converts into a Veteran Contract.

MYMs are not eligible to be loaded.

Eligibility:

Players acquired via the Auction or Pre-Season Waivers that were not given a contract before the contract deadline date.

The deadline for this is before the final game ends on Week 2 of the NFL Season.

Players acquired via Waivers during the season, either from their own pickup or after trading for said player. The deadline is 2 weeks from the date the player was acquired from Waivers.

Contract Extensions:

Eligibility:

Players entering the final year of their contract are eligible for an extension up until the contract deadline date.

Players acquired via the Rookie Draft or Preseason Waivers that were not given a contract by the deadline date nor given a MYM by NFL Week 2 may be extended by the end of Week 4.

Players acquired via trade during the season in the final year of their contract must be extended within 4 weeks of acquisition.

Extension Terms:

Extensions may be for 1 or 2 years.

Extensions reset the contract's Total Contract Value (TCV) and guarantees.

Future AAV is increased based on the extension schedule:

Schedule 1 (QB/RB/WR/TE):

1 Year: +$10,000

2 Years: +$20,000

Schedule 2 (DB/LB/DL/Kickers - IDP & Special Teams):

1 Year: +$3,000

2 Years: +$5,000

Examples:

Schedule 1 Example:

A player with a 1-year contract remaining at a $17K AAV extends for 1 year. The new AAV for the extended year would be $27K, resulting in a Total Contract Value (TCV) of $44K.

Schedule 2 Example:

A player with a 1-year contract remaining at a $10K AAV extends for 2 years. The new AAV for the extended years would be $20K, resulting in a Total Contract Value (TCV) of $50K.

9.5 Contract Guarantees & Cap Penalties

Current Rule (UPS Rule Book 2024.2)

Contract Guarantees:

The majority of contracts come with a 75% TCV Guarantee Requirement.

Contracts earn on a pro-rata basis over time, with 25% earned each month from October to December. Once the fantasy season is completed and the new season has been rolled over, the player will have earned 100% of that prior year's salary.

This schedule does not apply to:

Veteran & WW Contracts: where the original contract length is 1 year and the salary is less than $5K. These contracts have a 0% guarantee, representing a cap-free cut eligible player.

Taxi Squad Eligible Players: No guarantee on their contract until promoted to the active roster.

WW Contracts with a Salary of $5K or more: These contracts are 65% earned, resulting in a 35% cap penalty if dropped before the rollover to the next season.

Cap Penalties:

Calculated as (TCV * 75%) - Salary Earned.

Cap penalties that occur after the start of the auction are accrued and applied to the following season. Penalties occurring during the offseason up until the Roster Lock Date are applied to the current season.

Example of Cap Penalties:

Year 1 Example:

A contract with a TCV of $100K ($75K guaranteed) that is cut in September incurs a $75K cap penalty. If cut in October, the player would have earned 25% of Year 1 Salary (18.75K), resulting in a total cap penalty of $56.25K.

Year 2 Example:

If the same player is not cut in Year 1 but is cut in October of Year 2, the cap penalty would be calculated based on the guaranteed amount remaining from Year 1 plus any additional guarantees from Year 2.
