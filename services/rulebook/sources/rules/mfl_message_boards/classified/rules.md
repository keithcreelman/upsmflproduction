# MFL Message Board Rules (Classified)

Source: `rules/mfl_message_boards/manual/2010_messageboard.txt`

## 2010
- Free Agency Period added for Sundays: 6:00 AM–1:00 PM ET, free agents only, no injury stipulation, $1 acquisition salary per player, does not affect blind-bid balance, cap penalties still apply. Announced Oct 6–9, 2010; poll passed 8–2 (75% threshold) and went into effect Sunday Oct 10, 2010.
- Waiver processing schedule: waivers processed at 3:00 AM (day after Aug 31 notice) and again Sundays at 3:00 AM.
- Waiver bid structure clarification: multiple targets require separate bids; if a higher-priority claim wins, lower-priority bids tied to the same claim do not process. One-time ruling: poll “Should Ryan get B. Scott for $2” passed 9–1 (Sep 15, 2010).
- Playoffs and payouts: 12-team league at $50 per team plus $50 site fee = $550 prize pool; payouts $300 (1st), $175 (2nd), $75 (3rd). Two division winners receive first-round byes, best overall record is #1 seed, next four best records fill remaining playoff spots. Playoffs run Weeks 14–16 (Oct 14, 2010).
- Doubleheaders in use (DH Weekend #1 noted Sep 22, 2010).
- IR eligibility: IR spot for injured players only; off-field issues are not eligible and players will be removed (Sep 18, 2010).
- Scoring: return TDs do not count toward RB/WR scoring; only D/ST would receive return TD points (Sep 18, 2010). Poll on return TDs shows 8–1 in favor of counting, but decision at the time was to exclude.
- Roster/Salary adjustment: non-votes counted as “yes,” Gostkowski salary set to $5 and an extra roster spot added; owners could pick up the extra player on waivers (Sep 8, 2010). Poll referenced: “Is everyone ok with me fixing Gostowski’s Salary?” (multi-choice).

## 2011
- Contract posting deadline: max six 3-year deals; contracts due by midnight Sep 6, 2011 or all players default to 1-year deals.
- Initial draft/auction: nominations required daily; first 7 days limited to one nomination per day, then 7 days at two per day, then open nominations until rosters filled. Bids start at $1K in $1K increments; cap $275K; min roster 20, max 23. Nomination timer is 24 hours and resets if a max bid is exceeded.
- Auction nominations: ineligible nominations included Mesko, Foxworth, Beason, Avant, and Greg Lewis; invalid nominations were rescinded and returned to the pool. Bidding mistakes corrected after auction close to avoid seeing other bids.
- League dues enforcement: unpaid teams locked out of lineup and transactions; dues required within two weeks of Oct 16, 2011 or replacement; competition committee maintains lineups with only necessary pickups.
- Drafted players minimum contract: any drafted player (rookie, FA auction, etc.) is locked into at least a 1-year deal; dropping before contract assignment treats player as 1-year for cap purposes. No drops allowed until the draft concludes; contract length due by season start (9/8/2011) for that year.
- Taxi squad: not effective in 2011; only players drafted in the NFL Rookie Draft (2nd round+ in next July) are eligible.
- Cap-penalty exemptions: no cap penalty if total contract value is under $5K; retire/death releases with no cap penalty and player becomes free if unretires.
- Holdouts/jail/suspensions: season-long suspension/jail (declared pre-season) treated like retirement—owner may cut with no penalty but forfeits rights; if retained, player counts on roster at 50% salary and contract does not roll forward. Holdouts starting Week 1 get 25% cap relief while still counting on roster; by Week 8 owner must choose either full cap relief with no rollforward (player ineligible to start if he returns; extra roster spot granted) or rollforward (no further relief; player eligible on return). No relief if you pick up a player already retired/jailed/suspended.
- Cap penalty math: penalties based on total contract value (years × salary), not years + salary; team total rounded to nearest $1K after all cuts; contracts under $4K excluded from cap-hit totals.
- Franchise/transition tags: if retained by the tagging owner, player is 1-year only and cannot be retagged the following year. Qualifying offer should be the average of top 5 (franchise) or top 10 (transition) at the position and must exceed prior salary; prior-year salaries included in the average. Debate/poll pending on whether acquiring owner can choose which picks to give (including for transition tags) and whether a multi-year deal can be awarded after outbidding.
- Extensions: minimum raise remains $10K for 1-year and $20K for 2-year extensions; players acquired with 1 year left can be extended within the first 2 weeks of acquisition. In-season extensions take effect after the season; if a player is cut before the raise year, cap penalty includes the extension amount. Alternative raise formulas (20%/40% for higher-priced players) and IDP/ST extension bumps were proposed.
- Scoring: punts inside the 20 are worth 2 points. +1 point for tackles for loss (approved). Punter scoring change proposed (1 pt per 40 yards; inside-20 from 2 to 4 plus avg bonus) but in-season change rejected for 2011. Return-yardage scoring and Week 17 Pro Bowl format were proposed.
- Front/back loaded contracts approved (details/limits TBD); does not apply to rookie contracts.
- Off-season cap penalty: 10% removed from final ballot; final outcome confirmed as 20% (same as in-season).
- Off-season cuts: owners cutting a player in the off-season are forbidden from bidding on that player in the next free agent auction.
- Free agency/waivers: blind-bid waivers run Wed/Thu/Sat/Sun AM; Sunday FCFS free agency retained; no Thursday-only free-agent window; free agency not allowed on Mondays. Players remain locked until waivers clear; unlocked players reset to 1-year at blind-bid value. Tie-breakers for equal bids use worst-to-first (overall win %, then points, H2H, power rank); conditional bid priority applies. Special Week 16 free agency window (10 AM–1 PM) with no blind-bid run after.
- Draft order: NFL-style 1–12 in every round (no snake).
- Playoff-period adds: players picked up during playoffs are not eligible for extensions/franchising and are removed after the season.
- IR/return yardage/pro-bowl: proposals to allow OUT players on IR with cap relief, allow return yardage, and a Week 17 Pro Bowl format were pending. Return yardage did not start in 2011; it begins in 2012.

## 2012
- Rookie contracts remained 2 years for 2012; league vote moved rookie contracts to 3 years starting in 2013.
- Tie handling changed: bench-player (non-QB) tiebreaker adopted for regular season/playoffs (8–4 vote). This rule was later removed prior to 2025 by commissioner ruling.
- Off-season extensions: extension years only (no immediate salary increase year-of-extension) passed 11–1; this became the governing timing rule.
- IDP minimum rejected: proposal to require 8 IDP roster minimum failed 3–8; only starting lineup IDP requirements remained.
- Auction nominations: 2 nominations per day until rosters fill approved (7–4), with nomination cadence later treated as flexible per auction conditions.
- First blind-bid waivers: 2012 poll selected first Wednesday after the auction (7–5). Waiver timing later varied year to year.
- Player-drop reversal precedent: Philip Rivers drop reversal passed 9–3; established precedent for reversing immediate drops when reported promptly (distinct from waiver-bid mistakes).

## 2013
- Polls for rules were for 2014 implementation; one exception noted: the back-loaded cap-hit change was to go into effect immediately.
- Off-season cut deadline set to July 31; no drops allowed until after the auction; list of ineligible auction bids to be published.
- Contract deadlines: FA contracts, off-season extensions, and roster cut-downs due end of day September 1.
- Roster deadline enforcement: if over the roster limit at deadline, last acquired player removed (Daniel Thomas example).
- Restructure guidance: restructures should be tied to extensions and follow a fixed structure (front/back load or even split); arbitrary “signing bonus” numbers discouraged.
- Trade deadline reaffirmed as Week 12 kickoff.
- Season start moved from Feb 1 to Mar 1 starting 2014; IR activation deadline pushed later in off-season; off-season has no salary cap.
- Trade processing changed: no trade-vote requirement; commissioner processes trades. Veto only if two owners allege collusion within 48 hours (poll needed with 5 collusion votes). Trades involving starters must process 4 hours before kickoff. MFL setting added to block trades if any player has already kicked off.
- Rookie draft moved to July 1 (2013 season).
- Lineup compliance: warnings and escalating fines for starting bye/OUT/Doubtful players; repeat violations can lead to league vote and removal.
- Roster-min enforcement: penalty for failing to reach roster minimum was discussed (fine or cap hit). Future enforcement announced: loss of 1st-round pick plus $10 fine; dropping players before waivers opened carried a $10 fine.
- Standings tie-breaker order confirmed: Overall win %, Divisional win %, Points For, Head-to-Head, All-Play, Power Rank, Coin Flip.
- Competition committee proposals (Dec 2011): codify lineup-submission protocol for missed lineups; adjust playoff seeding tiebreakers (H2H/All-Play/PF/PWR/Coin Flip); shift divisional tiebreakers to favor H2H; change Tuesday waiver run to 7 AM; enforce trade-response strikes and require email notifications for board/trades/polls.
- League vote proposals (Dec 2011): expand roster size by 3 with minimum 8 IDP and draft max 31; add formations; allow OUT players on IR; revise extension raise formula; change punter scoring; add return-yardage scoring and Week 17 Pro Bowl.
- Off-season definition clarified as Feb 1–Jul 31; IR activation deadline moved to Jan 31.
