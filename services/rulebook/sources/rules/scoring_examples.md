## EX-SCORING-1 | Current Punter Scoring
Kind: math_example
Topic: Scoring & Starters
Applies To: S-SPECIAL-TEAMS,S-CURRENT-SCORING
Keywords: punter,pi,any,special-teams

Scenario:
Owner wants to understand how a punter earns points in the current setup.

Inputs:
- Punts inside the 20: 2
- Average punt yardage: 50.3

Calculation:
- PI points: 2 x 4 = 8
- ANY bonus for 50.00-59.99: 3
- Total from these events: 11 points

Outcome:
A punter can produce meaningful points from both placement and average distance.

Why it matters:
The league keeps punters as a real lineup position, so owners need clear math for weekly lineup decisions.

## EX-SCORING-2 | Return Yardage
Kind: math_example
Topic: Scoring & Starters
Applies To: S-RETURN-YARDAGE,S-CURRENT-SCORING
Keywords: return-yardage,uy,ky

Scenario:
Owner evaluates a player with both punt-return and kick-return work.

Inputs:
- Punt return yards: 40
- Kick return yards: 80

Calculation:
- UY scoring: 40 x 0.05 = 2.0
- KY scoring: 80 x 0.025 = 2.0
- Combined return-yardage points: 4.0

Outcome:
Return specialists can add real lineup value even without a touchdown.

Why it matters:
This explains why some depth players have extra weekly utility in the UPS format.
